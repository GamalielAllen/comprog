Frequently Asked Questions

What does a Hydraulic Engineer do?

A Hydraulic Engineer designs and manages systems that control and distribute water, including dams, canals, pipelines, drainage systems, and flood control structures.

Why is hydraulic engineering important?

Hydraulic engineering ensures efficient water supply, flood prevention, irrigation management, and protection of communities from water-related hazards.

What principles are used in hydraulic engineering?

Hydraulic engineering applies principles of fluid mechanics, hydrology, and engineering design to analyze and manage water systems.

  • Water distribution systems
  • Flood management
  • Drainage and irrigation systems
